格助詞{かくじょし}（ＣＡＳＥ　ＰＡＲＴＩＣＬＥＳ）

In English, word order is used to define what grammatical role (a.k.a case) a word has. In Japanese, Case Particles are used to identify the case of the noun that comes before it, for example: subject, direct object, loaction, destination, etc

並立助詞{へいれつじょし}（ＰＡＲＡＬＬＥＬ　ＰＡＲＴＩＣＬＥＳ）

Parallel particles are used put nouns and noun phrases in parallel (thus the name), and in English they mostly translate to words similar to "and" and "or"

終助詞{しゅうじょし}（ＳＥＮＴＥＮＣＥ－ＥＮＤＩＮＧ　ＰＡＲＴＩＣＬＥＳ）

Sentence-ending particles are gramatical kana placed at the end of sentences to show its force, such as emphasis, confirmation, questioning, emotion, etc

間投助詞{かんとうじょし}（ＩＮＴＥＲＪＥＣＴＯＲＹ　ＰＡＲＴＩＣＬＥＳ）

Interjectory particles are used to show emotion, emphasis, or gain attention from the listener, primarly focusing on feeling and conversational tone rather than gramatical structures

副助詞{ふくじょし}（ＡＤＶＥＲＢＩＡＬ　ＰＡＲＴＩＣＬＥＳ）

Adverbial particles act similarly to adverbs (thus the name) by modifiy words or phrases to express concepts like limits, addition, emphasis, etc

係助詞{かかりじょし}（ＢＩＮＤＩＮＧ　ＰＡＲＴＩＣＬＥＳ）

Binding particles attach/bind to words and phrases and highlight them, creating emphasis, contrast, restriction, and overall affecting how the sentence is interpreted or structured

接続助詞{せつぞくじょし}（ＣＯＮＪＵＮＣＴＩＶＥ　ＰＡＲＴＩＣＬＥＳ）

Conjunctive particles connect one clause to another and shows the relationship between the them, whether it is in contrast, cause or reason, time sequence, condition, etc
